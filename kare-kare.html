Kare-Kare Recipe

A rich and savory Filipino oxtail stew made with peanut sauce, vegetables, and bagoong on the side.

Ingredients:
  • 1 kg oxtail (or beef shank), cut into pieces
  • 1/2 kg tripes (optional), cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup annatto seeds (atsuete), soaked in warm water
  • 1 large eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 banana heart (puso ng saging), sliced (optional)
  • 1/2 cup bagoong (fermented shrimp paste)
  • 5 cups beef broth (or water for boiling the oxtail)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. In a large pot, bring the beef broth to a boil. Add the oxtail and tripes, if using, and simmer for about 2 hours, or until the meat becomes tender.
  2. While the meat is cooking, sauté garlic and onion in oil in a separate pan until fragrant and soft.
  3. Once the meat is tender, remove it from the pot and set aside. Strain the broth and set aside as well.
  4. In the same pan, add the sautéed garlic and onion mixture to the pot with the beef broth, followed by the peanut butter and annatto water.
  5. Stir everything well and bring to a simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to combine.
  6. Return the oxtail (and tripes, if using) to the pot. Add the vegetables and cook until tender, about 10-15 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the consistency of the sauce by adding more broth if needed.
  8. Serve hot with a side of bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) and enjoy!
